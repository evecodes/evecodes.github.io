Koi Botanical Gardens

Welcome

Welcome to Koi Botanical Gardens. Plan your visit, learn about our gardens, and escape the humdrum of daily life. Koi Botanical Gardens was founded in the 19th century as a safe haven for endangered plants, and remains a place of preservation to this day, with a collection of over 4000 species.

Our gardens

Rose Garden

Its shape modeled after the Grandiflora rose, this garden is filled to the brim of different types of roses. From the rare Wild roses, to the fragrant Old Garden rose, and let's not forget about the dazzling Tea roses. One of the main centerpieces is the rose tunnel, decorated and entwined by the Climbing rose. Have fun exploring this fairytale experience.

Koi Garden

The oldest garden, which started as a koi pond, has grown to a collection of ponds with each their own unique water wildlife habitat. From a mountain spring to a marsh landscape, dive from world to world, and get submerged in the atmosphere. At various points through the garden there are benches accompanied by wonderful vistas.

Wild flower Garden

Ever wanted to see what the flowers look like from across the ocean? This garden will take you on a vibrant journey through multiple countries. Thanks to careful planning and a uniquely designed layout of various plant species, this garden blooms during every season. Enjoy the sights and colours, it will sure be a treat!

Tearoom

Need a place for refreshments during your visit? Visit our tea room during your stay. It's located in the old greenhouse beside the koi garden.

The greenhouse was founded at the end of the 19th century to nurture the rare seeds and plants that were brought in from overseas. Nowadays it's open to the public and has several areas you can explore.

On the menu are freshly made meals with ingredients from the gardens. You can choose from a wide selection of items. A hearty lunch, or go for a coffee with cake, whichever suits your fancy. We also serve high-tea arrangements.

The tea room is available for group accommodations. There are indoor and outdoor spots to choose from. For group visits, please send a reservation email, and we'll book you a spot.

The tea room is open daily, from 10am to 4pm.
